# Tea Terminology Glossary

## Tea Types & Processing

**Camellia sinensis**
The tea plant. All true teas (white, green, oolong, black, pu-erh, yellow) come from this plant.

**Camellia sinensis var. sinensis**
Chinese tea plant variety. Smaller leaves, more cold-hardy. Produces more delicate teas.

**Camellia sinensis var. assamica**
Assam tea plant variety. Larger leaves, tropical climate. Produces robust, malty teas.

**Oxidation**
The enzymatic browning of tea leaves when exposed to air. Key factor in tea processing that determines tea type.

**Withering**
First step in tea processing where leaves lose moisture and become pliable.

**Rolling**
Shaping tea leaves and breaking cell walls to release enzymes for oxidation.

**Firing/Fixing**
Heating tea leaves to stop oxidation and preserve desired characteristics.

**Fermentation**
In tea: either enzymatic oxidation (black tea) or microbial fermentation (pu-erh).

## Tea Categories

**White Tea**
Minimally processed tea, made from young buds and leaves. Low oxidation (5-10%).

**Green Tea**
Unoxidized tea. Leaves are heated soon after picking to prevent oxidation.

**Yellow Tea**
Rare tea with slight oxidation (10-20%). Undergoes slow drying/yellowing process.

**Oolong Tea**
Partially oxidized tea (12-85%). Complex processing creates unique flavor profiles.

**Black Tea**
Fully oxidized tea (90-100%). Called "red tea" (hongcha) in China.

**Pu-erh Tea**
Post-fermented tea from Yunnan. Can be raw (sheng) or ripe (shou).

**Dark Tea**
Category of post-fermented teas including pu-erh, Liu Bao, and others.

## Brewing Terms

**Steeping**
Immersing tea leaves in hot water to extract flavors and compounds.

**Infusion**
A single steeping of tea leaves. Many teas can be infused multiple times.

**Reinfusion/Resteepin**
Brewing the same tea leaves multiple times, common with oolong and pu-erh.

**Gongfu Brewing**
Traditional Chinese method using small teapot, many short infusions with high leaf-to-water ratio.

**Western Brewing**
Common Western method using lower leaf ratio, longer steeping times, fewer infusions.

**Flash Rinse**
Quick initial rinse of tea leaves (5-10 seconds) to "wake up" the tea and remove dust.

## Tea Components

**Catechins**
Antioxidant compounds, especially abundant in green tea. Include EGCG.

**Theanine (L-theanine)**
Amino acid unique to tea. Promotes relaxation without drowsiness.

**Caffeine**
Natural stimulant in tea. Content varies by tea type and brewing method.

**Theaflavins**
Compounds formed during oxidation, contribute to black tea's color and flavor.

**Polyphenols**
Antioxidants in tea with health benefits. Includes catechins and flavonoids.

**Tannins**
Astringent compounds that give tea body and complexity.

## Tea Grades & Terms

**Flush**
Harvest season. First flush (spring) is often most prized.

**Plucking Standard**
Which leaves are picked. "One bud two leaves" is common quality standard.

**Orthodox Processing**
Traditional tea processing methods maintaining whole or partially whole leaves.

**CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl)**
Modern processing method producing small, uniform tea particles. Common for tea bags.

**Tippy**
Tea containing many golden or silver tips (young buds). Indicates quality.

**Pekoe**
Term from Chinese "bai hao" (white down). Refers to young tea leaves.

**Orange Pekoe (OP)**
Grade of black tea. Refers to leaf size, not flavor or orange.

**FTGFOP**
"Finest Tippy Golden Flowery Orange Pekoe" - highest grade black tea.

**Single Estate**
Tea from one specific tea garden or plantation.

**Blend**
Tea mixed from multiple origins, types, or with added ingredients.

## Cultivation Terms

**Terroir**
Environmental factors (soil, climate, altitude) affecting tea character.

**Cultivar**
Specific cultivated variety of tea plant with distinct characteristics.

**Elevation**
Height above sea level. High-grown teas (above 1000m) often more complex.

**Shade-Grown**
Tea grown under covers to increase chlorophyll and theanine. Used for gyokuro, matcha.

**Organic**
Tea grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers.

**Fair Trade**
Certification ensuring fair prices and conditions for tea workers.

## Specialized Teas

**Matcha**
Powdered green tea, traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremony.

**Sencha**
Most common Japanese green tea, steamed and rolled.

**Gyokuro**
Premium Japanese shade-grown green tea. Sweet and complex.

**Longjing (Dragon Well)**
Famous Chinese pan-fired green tea from Zhejiang.

**Da Hong Pao**
Legendary Chinese rock oolong from Wuyi Mountains.

**Darjeeling**
"Champagne of teas" from Darjeeling region, India.

**Assam**
Robust, malty black tea from Assam region, India.

**Ceylon**
Tea from Sri Lanka (formerly Ceylon). Bright, brisk character.

**Keemun**
Chinese black tea from Anhui. Wine-like, slightly smoky.

## Tea Culture

**Gongfu Cha**
Chinese tea ceremony focused on skill in brewing.

**Chanoyu**
Japanese tea ceremony emphasizing harmony, respect, purity, tranquility.

**Chai**
Spiced milk tea popular in India and neighboring regions.

**Tisane/Herbal Tea**
Infusion made from herbs, flowers, or fruits. Not from Camellia sinensis.

**Tea Master**
Expert in tea selection, preparation, and ceremony.

**Tea Tasting**
Systematic evaluation of tea's appearance, aroma, flavor, and mouthfeel.

## Storage Terms

**Airtight**
Sealed container preventing air exposure. Important for tea storage.

**Aging**
Intentional long-term storage to develop flavors. Common with pu-erh.

**Cake/Beeng**
Compressed tea, traditionally 357g. Common for pu-erh.

**Brick**
Rectangular compressed tea form.

**Tin**
Traditional tea storage container, often decorative.

## Health & Chemistry

**Antioxidants**
Compounds that protect cells from damage. Tea is rich in antioxidants.

**EGCG (Epigallocatechin Gallate)**
Most studied catechin in green tea with numerous health benefits.

**Fluoride**
Mineral in tea that supports dental health.

**Tannins**
Astringent plant compounds. Can interfere with iron absorption.

**Theophylline**
Compound in tea with bronchodilator properties.

## Modern Tea Terms

**Cold Brew**
Steeping tea in cold water for extended time (4-12 hours).

**Kombucha**
Fermented tea beverage made with SCOBY (symbiotic culture of bacteria and yeast).

**Bubble Tea/Boba**
Taiwanese tea-based drink with tapioca pearls.

**RTD (Ready-to-Drink)**
Bottled or canned tea beverages.

**Nitro Tea**
Cold tea infused with nitrogen for creamy texture.

## Quality Indicators

**Aroma**
Scent of dry leaves, wet leaves, and brewed tea. Key quality indicator.

**Liquor**
The brewed tea liquid (not the alcoholic beverage).

**Mouthfeel**
Texture and body of tea in the mouth.

**Astringency**
Drying, puckering sensation from tannins.

**Umami**
Savory, brothy flavor common in high-quality Japanese greens.

**Hui Gan**
"Returning sweetness" - pleasant aftertaste in quality tea.

**Cha Qi**
"Tea energy" - the feeling or effect experienced when drinking quality tea.

---

*This glossary covers essential tea terminology. Tea culture spans thousands of years and countless regions, with many more specialized terms used by enthusiasts and professionals.*
