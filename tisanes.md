# Tisane Explorer Documentation

**A reference for 100+ herbs from global traditions, 35+ manufacturers, historical context, geographic regions, and safety information.**

---

## Executive summary

This documentation provides the foundational data for a the tisane explorer application. The research covers **100+ individual tisane varieties** from Western herbalism, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, African, South American, Middle Eastern, and Indigenous traditions, along with **35+ manufacturers**, detailed geographic production data, 5,000+ years of history, and thorough safety/regulatory information.

The global herbal tea market reached approximately **$3.87-4.01 billion in 2024**, projected to grow to **$5.36-7.35 billion by 2032-2035**. Chamomile dominates at 34% market share, followed by peppermint and ginger. Gut and digestive health teas lead the functional category at 40%.

#### Traditional TCM formulas

**Ba Bao Cha (Eight Treasures Tea 八宝茶)**
- **Origin**: Late Qing Dynasty, created for Empress Dowager Cixi
- **Ingredients**: Jujube dates, Goji berries, Longan, Chrysanthemum, Ginseng, Hawthorn, Tangerine peel, Licorice, Rock sugar
- **Properties**: Caffeine-free, balanced, promotes digestion, circulation, harmony
- **Cultural Significance**: Hospitality drink; Mid-Autumn Festival accompaniment

**Liang Cha (Cooling Teas 凉茶)**
- **Origin**: Guangdong Province; UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage (2006)
- **Purpose**: Cool internal "Heat" (yeet hay/熱氣); treat early illness symptoms
- **Famous Varieties**: 24 Flavors Tea (most popular), Five Flowers Tea, Sugar Cane Cogon Grass Root Tea

#### Key Ayurvedic formulas

**CCF Tea (Cumin-Coriander-Fennel)**
- **Ingredients**: Equal parts cumin, coriander, fennel seeds
- **Properties**: Tridoshic (suitable for all doshas)
- **Benefits**: Supports digestion, reduces gas/bloating, enhances nutrient absorption
- **Preparation**: 1 tsp total per cup; steep 5-10 minutes or simmer 5 minutes

**Masala Chai**
- **Base Spices**: Green cardamom, cinnamon (Ceylon), fresh ginger, cloves, black peppercorns
- **Preparation**: Crush spices, boil in water, add tea, add milk (1:1 ratio), sweeten
- **Important**: Boil ginger before adding milk to prevent curdling

**Kadha/Kashayam (Immune Decoction)**
- **Core Ingredients**: Tulsi (4-5 leaves), ginger (1 inch), black peppercorns (3-4), turmeric (¼ tsp), cinnamon, cloves (2)
- **Preparation**: Boil, simmer until reduced by half (8-10 min), add honey when warm

#### Indigenous traditions

**Kava (Piper methysticum)**
- **Origin**: South Pacific Islands (Fiji, Vanuatu, Tonga, Samoa, Hawaii)
- **Uses**: Ceremonial drink, anxiolytic, muscle relaxant
- **Safety**: **HIGH RISK** - Reports of hepatotoxicity (liver damage), including transplants and deaths. Banned/restricted in Germany, France, Switzerland, Australia, Canada at various times. Traditional water extracts appear safer than commercial solvent extracts.
- **Caffeine**: None

**Lemon Myrtle (Backhousia citriodora)**
- **Origin**: Queensland, Australia
- **Traditional Uses**: Aboriginal Australians used for healing for thousands of years; natural sedative, antimicrobial
- **Key Compounds**: Highest citral purity (90-98%)
- **Flavor**: Strong lemony with menthol notes
- **Caffeine**: None

### 5.2 High-risk herbs (avoid or use extreme caution)

| Herb | Risk Level | Primary Concern | Key Warning |
|------|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Comfrey** | VERY HIGH | Pyrrolizidine alkaloids | AVOID internal use; liver toxicity, carcinogenic |
| **Pennyroyal** | VERY HIGH | Pulegone toxicity | Deaths reported; abortifacient |
| **Ephedra** | VERY HIGH | Cardiovascular events | FDA BANNED (2004) |
| **Kava** | HIGH | Hepatotoxicity | Liver failure, transplants; restricted in many countries |
| **Licorice** | HIGH (high doses) | Glycyrrhizin | Hypertension, hypokalemia; limit 4 weeks, 1-4g/day max |
| **St. John's Wort** | HIGH (interactions) | CYP3A4 inducer | MOST drug interactions of any herb |
| **Chaparral** | HIGH | NDGA | Acute hepatitis |
| **Germander** | HIGH | Diterpenoids | Hepatotoxicity; banned in several countries |

### 5.3 Major drug interactions

| Medication Class | High-Risk Herbs | Effect |
|------------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Anticoagulants (Warfarin)** | St. John's Wort | REDUCES effect (under-anticoagulation) |
| | Ginkgo, Garlic, Ginger | Increases bleeding risk |
| **Immunosuppressants** | St. John's Wort | Dramatically reduces levels (rejection risk) |
| **SSRIs/Antidepressants** | St. John's Wort | Serotonin syndrome risk |
| **Oral Contraceptives** | St. John's Wort | REDUCES efficacy (pregnancy risk) |
| **CNS Depressants** | Valerian, Kava, Passionflower | Additive sedation |
| **Diabetes Medications** | Fenugreek, Bitter Melon | Additive hypoglycemic effects |

### 5.4 Special populations

**Pregnancy - AVOID:**
- Pennyroyal, Blue/Black Cohosh, Dong Quai, Mugwort, Licorice (high doses), Kava, Ephedra, Comfrey, Hibiscus

**Pregnancy - Generally Safe (moderate amounts):**
- Ginger (for nausea), Peppermint, Rose hip

**Pre-surgery (stop 2 weeks before):**
- Garlic, Ginkgo, Ginseng, Ginger (bleeding risk)
- Valerian, Kava (anesthesia interactions)
- St. John's Wort (cardiovascular collapse risk)

**Children:**
- Limited clinical data; dilute teas preferred
- Chamomile and fennel traditionally used for infant colic
- Avoid all high-risk herbs
